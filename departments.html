Havenwatch

Overview

Havenwatch is organized into departments that separate duties and maintain checks. Departments are expected to coordinate through SOPs and reporting lines; some roles may serve in reserve/advisory capacity.

4.1 Case Intake, Review & Triage Department

Purpose: Acts as front-line intake and filtering.

Responsibilities:

  • Receive reports and tipline submissions.
  • Initial and secondary evidence screening for eligibility.
  • Compile structured case packets for operations or legal review.
  • Follow-up with reporters (securely) where needed.
  • Determine case outcome states (accept, request-more-info, reject, escalate).

Key Roles: Intake Reviewer; Intake Lead (senior triage authority).

Notes: Communication skills are mandatory; evidence validation is performed here before cases progress.

4.2 Digital Investigations & Analysis Department

Purpose: Conduct structured OSINT on verified, actionable cases.

Responsibilities:

  • OSINT investigations to map context and corroborating indicators.
  • Timeline reconstruction and artifact documentation.
  • Support Intake and Legal teams with analysis outputs.

Key Roles: Digital Investigator; Analysis Specialist.

Limitations: No HUMINT unless explicitly authorized; no non-open-source methods; avoid invasive or speculative techniques.

4.3 Legal, Policy & External Relations Department

Purpose: Ensure legal compliance and coordinate external handovers.

Responsibilities:

  • Legal screening for jurisdictional and evidentiary issues.
  • Approval and packaging of cyber-tips for handover.
  • Draft and maintain SOPs, policy documents, and standards.
  • Maintain relationships with law enforcement, NGOs, and platforms.

Key Roles: Field Legal Reviewer; Senior Legal Reviewer; Policy & Standards Advisor; External Relations & Cybertipping Specialist.

Notes: Legal advisors may be advisory/reserve; not all roles require weekly activity.

4.4 Operations & Case Management Department

Purpose: Maintain internal continuity, manage access and workloads.

Responsibilities:

  • Case assignment, reassignment, and progress tracking.
  • Monitor volunteer availability and workload limits.
  • Enforce inactivity and compliance policies.
  • Manage access to shared infrastructure and oversee internal complaints.

Key Roles: Operations Coordinator; Case Management Officer; Internal Compliance Officer.

4.5 Communications, Education & Outreach Department

Purpose: Produce prevention-focused public communication.

Responsibilities:

  • Create educational and awareness content based on evidence and best practice.
  • Research emerging trends and publish summaries (legal-reviewed).
  • Coordinate outreach and partnerships (future expansion).

Key Roles: Content Editors & Publishers; Researchers; Outreach (future).

Limitations: All public content must be legally reviewed; no sensationalism or exposure culture.

5 Programs — HIRP (Human Interaction Risk Program)

Status: Conditional / Restricted

Purpose: Allows limited HUMINT/directed interaction only under strict conditions and explicit approvals.

Activation Criteria:

  • Actionable case with identified person of interest (POI).
  • Clear evidence of exploitation or grooming.
  • Legal approval and narrowly defined objective.

Restricted Practices: No entrapment; no fishing for evidence; no low-evidence interaction; escalation beyond scope is prohibited.

6–8 Roles, Volunteer Model & Case Handling Principles

Roles & Participation: Case handlers are primary/assistant roles with Ops oversight. Legal, Ops, and Communications have role-specific restrictions to avoid conflicts.

Department membership: One primary department; one secondary allowed if capacity permits; cross-training encouraged.

Volunteer expectations: Minimum 1–2 hours or one task each week. Inactivity (2 weeks) may lead to temporary pause for security.

Case handling principles: Evidence-based decisions; no speculative escalations; clear outcome states; legal review for cyber-tips; resource-aware prioritization.

9–11 Funding, Neutrality & Governance

Funding status: No guaranteed funding; volunteer-based; minimal infrastructure costs. Financial principles: transparency; no personal profit; funds used for infrastructure, awareness, training.

Neutrality & Independence: No political endorsements; no vigilantism; cooperate lawfully while preserving independence from LEAs.

Amendments & Governance: Charter amendments by leadership with committee review; policies and SOPs derive authority from the Charter; conflicts resolved in favor of child safety & legal compliance.
