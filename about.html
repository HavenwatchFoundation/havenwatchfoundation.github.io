Mission & purpose

Havenwatch Foundation for Child Protection is a volunteer-based organization focused on identifying, documenting, and responsibly reporting online sexual exploitation involving minors. We help reports move from scattered evidence into clear, structured, lawful reporting pathways. Our work prioritizes accuracy, restraint, and accountability — not publicity or confrontation.

Status (Jan 2026): Havenwatch is in the process of registering as a formal non-profit organization. Until registration completes we continue to operate under strict internal governance and professional standards.

Why Havenwatch exists

Online abuse and exploitation are frequently underreported or inconsistently handled because of:

  • Unclear reporting guidance
  • Poor evidence preservation
  • Fear of making mistakes when reporting
  • Unstructured or reckless volunteer approaches that waste effort or cause harm

Havenwatch addresses these gaps by providing a disciplined, evidence-focused process that prioritizes child safety, legal compliance, and careful handling over speed or attention.

What we do

We perform a small, well-defined set of activities designed to support lawful reporting:

  • Receive and review reports of suspected online child exploitation
  • Preserve and document digital evidence in a structured manner
  • Conduct open-source analysis on verified, actionable cases
  • Review cases for legal compliance before escalation
  • Submit lawful reports or cyber-tips to appropriate entities
  • Produce education and prevention-focused awareness material

All actions follow internal standards emphasizing proportionality, documentation, and care for reporters and affected individuals.

What we do not do

To be clear about our boundaries, Havenwatch does not:

  • Conduct arrests, interrogations, or enforcement operations
  • Impersonate law enforcement or claim investigative authority
  • Engage in public exposures, harassment, doxxing, or online shaming
  • Use entrapment, fishing tactics, or other coercive practices
  • Guarantee specific outcomes for reports or cases
  • Act as a replacement for law enforcement or child protection agencies

Our role is to support lawful processes, not replace them.

Volunteer model & participation

Havenwatch is volunteer-run and role-based. Access to case materials and infrastructure is granted only when necessary for a defined responsibility and is paused when that role becomes inactive.

  • Participation is role-based with clear expectations and oversight.
  • Typical minimum contribution: 1–2 hours or one task per week for active roles.
  • Realistic workload limits and the ability to step back without penalty are built into the model.
  • Accounts inactive for two weeks may be temporarily paused to protect operational security.

Path toward non-profit status

Havenwatch is actively transitioning to a formally registered non-profit. This process includes establishing:

  • Formal governance structures and written policies
  • Operational standards and SOPs
  • Controlled access to infrastructure
  • Transparent financial principles
  • Clear separation of operational, legal, and communications functions

Until registration completes, we maintain internal discipline and documentation standards consistent with professional non-profit practice.

Core principles

Our daily work and long-term policies are guided by these principles:

  • Child safety first — protection of children outweighs publicity or recognition.
  • Legal compliance — all actions must align with applicable laws and reporting frameworks.
  • Non-vigilantism — no harassment, doxxing, or extrajudicial behavior.
  • Neutrality — no political or ideological agendas.
  • Proportionality — resources are allocated based on evidence and risk.
  • Trauma awareness — victims and reporters are handled with care and respect.
  • Operational integrity — accuracy, documentation, and accountability are mandatory.
