About — Organizational Charter (Draft)

Charter status: Draft Charter — Date: 27 December 2025 · Jurisdiction: Global, civilian-led

1. Identity & Purpose

1.1 Name
Havenwatch Foundation for Child Protection (HFCP) — “Havenwatch”.

1.2 Nature of the Organization
Havenwatch is a civilian-led, non-governmental, non-profit initiative dedicated to child protection in online environments. Havenwatch is not a law enforcement agency, does not claim police authority, and does not conduct arrests, prosecutions, or coercive actions. We operate independently while cooperating with recognized platforms, NGOs, and lawful reporting channels.

1.3 Mission Statement
Havenwatch’s mission is to:

  • Make reporting cases accessible across the internet.
  • Support lawful reporting and escalation pathways.
  • Promote ethical, evidence-based handling of reports.
  • Strengthen prevention and awareness through education and outreach.
  • Operate with professionalism, neutrality, and legal compliance.

1.4 Core Principles
We operate under: Child Safety First; Legal Compliance; Non-Vigilantism; Neutrality; Proportionality; Trauma Awareness; Operational Integrity.

2. Scope of Activities

2.1 What Havenwatch Does
Receiving and reviewing reports of suspected online child exploitation; evidence preservation and documentation; OSINT; legal screening; cyber-tipping and lawful handover; awareness and prevention-focused education.

2.2 What Havenwatch Does NOT Do
We do not conduct arrests or enforcement, impersonate law enforcement, engage in exposure or doxxing, use entrapment, guarantee outcomes, provide long-term counseling, or act as a replacement for law enforcement.

3–11: Structure, Programs, Principles & Governance (summary)

Havenwatch is organized into departments responsible for intake, analysis, legal review, operations, and communications. Programs (like HIRP) are conditional and strictly controlled. Volunteers follow a clear participation model with minimum activity expectations, inactivity handling, and role categories. Evidence-based decision-making, legal review of cyber-tips, and resource-aware prioritization guide case handling. Financially, Havenwatch operates on a volunteer model with no guaranteed funding; any funds are to be used transparently for infrastructure, training, and aid/awareness initiatives.

Neutrality and independence are central: we do not engage in political advocacy or vigilantism and maintain cooperation with lawful channels while preserving operational independence.

Important operational notes

Volunteer expectations: typical minimum contribution is 1–2 hours or one task per week. Accounts inactive for two weeks may be paused for security.

Legal & safety: All public content is reviewed by Legal. All cyber-tips are packaged and screened prior to handover. Trauma-aware handling is mandatory for reporters and victims.

Contact & reporting

Contact: havenwatchfoundation@proton.me

If you suspect child exploitation online, please report promptly to local law enforcement or to trusted hotlines such as the NCMEC CyberTipline: report.cybertip.org
